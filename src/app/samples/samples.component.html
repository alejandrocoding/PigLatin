See some of the examples of how this app should work.

1) For words that begin with consonant sounds, all letters before the initial vowel are placed at the end of the word sequence:

  • pig ig-pay
  • latin atin-lay
  • banana anana-bay
  • happy appy-hay
  • duck uck-day
  • me e-may
  • real eal-ray
  • will ill-way
  • wet et-way
  • too oo-tay

2) When words begin with consonant clusters (multiple consonants that form one sound), the whole sound is added to the end when speaking or writing:

  • cheers eers-chay
  • shesh esh-shay
  • smile ile-smay
  • string ing-stray
  • thanks anks-thay
  • stupid upid-stay
  • shrink ink-shray
  • strong ong-stray
  • troll oll-tray
  • spray ay-spray

3) For words that begin with vowel sounds:

  • eat eat-ay
  • omelet omelet-ay
  • egg egg-ay
  • explain explain-ay
  • until until-ay
  • angular angular-ay
  • is is-ay
  • only only-ay
  • always always-ay
  • amazing amazing-ay
